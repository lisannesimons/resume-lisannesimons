About

More About Me

When looking at other résumés, I often feel like everybody makes themselves out to be hardworking, quick learning, team players. Quite understandable, since it is difficult to leave a good impression when having to rely on written words. I therefore decided to join the crowd and use these cliché terms to give you the chance to get to know me a little before diving into my academic achievements and work experience. However, I hope that the impression you'll get of me will be shaped by my honesty.

Am I hardworking? I do think I am. Since I enjoy the feeling of joy that comes with success, I work hard for what I want or aim to achieve. Nevertheless, in the last couple of years it has become clear to me that to be concentrated and creative when I work, I also require time to reset. I try to do this on a daily basis by getting outside to go for a walk and by practicing yoga (or something like that, I am not very good yet). When I find the time, I also like to write fiction, and it is a dream of mine to become a published author one day.

I would not say that I am a quick learner. Learning takes time. Especially when it is something completely new. Even so, I do consider myself to be motivated and open to keep on developing myself. I am not afraid to take on a challenge and I often go the extra mile to upgrade or widen my skills. Nowadays, most knowledge is up for grasps, as long as you know where to look for it. Though I try to make use of this to figure out things on my own, I do know when to ask for help as well.

Finally, I would certainly not describe myself as a team player yet. In that statement, the yet is important. I am assertive, perfectionisitic, organized, and usually have a strong opinion on what I believe to be best. Though these traits are desirable in certain situations, it has made it difficult for me to collaborate with others in the past. At least, I am aware that bossy is not what I want to be. I like to think that my collaboration skills have already gotten better as I grew older, but I know that there is still room for improvement. Now, standing at the start of my working life, I am optimistic that, if given the chance, I will get the hang of it eventually.

Hopefully, you now have a first idea of who I am. Who knows, maybe we will get to know each other better soon! If you are interested in more information about me? Please use the contact form to get in touch!

Education

My Education

In the last grade of primary school, my teacher told me she thought I was aiming too high. Instead of lowering my ambitions, I decided to prove her wrong.

October 2018 - June 2020

RWTH Aachen University

Master Technical Communication Fundamentals of Computer Science

My master studies built further on the bachelor with the same name. For the computer science part, courses I attended include: Virtual Reality, Web-Mining, Social Computing, and Data Communication and Security. The communication science part of the master dealt with topics like Usability, User Diversity and Technology Acceptance and Communicative Usability. For my master's thesis I conducted a mixed methods empirical study in Germany and the Netherlands. The thesis was graded with an 1.0 (German grading system, equivalent to an A+) and was titled "The acceptance and communication of insulation boards produced by means of Carbon Capture and Utilization: a comparison between the Germans and the Dutch".

October 2015 - August 2018

RWTH Aachen University

Bachelor Technical Communication Fundamentals of Computer Science

In this interdisciplinary field of study, half of the classes taught me the fundamentals of computer science, and the other half communication science. I graduated within three years and was placed on the Dean's List twice. For my bachelor's thesis I carried out and reported an empirical quantitative study. The thesis was graded with an 1.0 (German grading system, equivalent to an A+) and was titled "The influence of user diversity on the awareness of filter bubbles regarding political opinion formation".

Want to learn more about this unique subject area? Visit the university's information page here.

August 2009 - June 2015

Charlemagne College

Bilingual University Preparatory Education (NL: tweetalig gymnasium)

After six years I graduated cum laude and was equipped with the necessary skills to attend a university, as well as a good mastery of the English language. In the Netherlands, bilingual secondary schools are regulated by Nuffic, a non-profit organisation that ensures the quality of the lessons and teachers. For me, following bilingual education meant that during the first half of secondary school, all classes, except for the languages, were taught in English. During the second half, lessons were taught in Dutch again, but I still had more hours of English a week, as well as additional projects and exams. Because of these, I was rewarded with several certificates: i.a., M15 English A Lang and Lit by the International Baccalaureate and English Language Advanced Subsidiary and Global Perspectives Advanced Subsidiary by Cambridge International Examinations.
