More About Me

Welcome to my resume website! I am delighted that you chose to visit. At this moment, I am looking for an opportunity to give something back to the community that recently welcomed me. Who knows, maybe I have the skills you need. Before diving into my academic achievements and work experience, I would like to introduce myself and my current situation.

In the summer of 2020, I attained my master's degree from RWTH Aachen University in Germany. Subsequently, I worked for a year as a research associate at the university's Chair for Communication Science and the Human-Computer Interaction Center. Through this job I gained more hands-on experience with human-centered research. The work included conducting two large-scale quantitative questionnaire studies from beginning to end. Together with my teammates, I designed and implemented the questionnaires, collected and screened the data, to then clean, analyze, and visualize it. Our findings were documented in several papers, one of which has already been published. To take a look at the paper, click here .

At the end of July 2021, I left Germany and moved to San Jose (CA, United States of America). The visa that allowed me to move here requires me to wait for employment authorization before I can put my knowledge into practice in a paid job. In the meantime, I would hate to let my time and expertise go to waste. That is why I am now looking for an opportunity to give something back to the community. Ideally, I would like to work on something related to user experience research or design. For example, I could help your organization gain insight into how your services are received or assist in improving the user-friendliness of your website, system, or forms.

Hopefully, you have gotten a first idea of who I am now. Maybe we will get to know each other better soon! If you could use my help or if you have any unanswered questions, please use the contact form to get in touch or email me directly.

My Education

In the last grade of primary school, my teacher told me she thought I was aiming too high. Instead of lowering my ambitions, I decided to prove her wrong.

October 2018 - June 2020

RWTH Aachen University

M.Sc. Technical Communication Fundamentals of Computer Science

My master's studies built further on the bachelor with the same name. For the computer science part, courses I attended include: Virtual Reality, Web-Mining, Social Computing, Data Communication and Security, and Efficient Algorithms. The communication science part of the master's dealt with topics like Usability, User Diversity and Technology Acceptance and Communicative Usability. For my master's thesis I conducted a mixed methods empirical study in Germany and the Netherlands. The thesis was graded with an 1.0 (German grading system, equivalent to an A+) and was titled "The acceptance and communication of insulation boards produced by means of Carbon Capture and Utilization: a comparison between the Germans and the Dutch".

October 2015 - August 2018

RWTH Aachen University

B.Sc. Technical Communication Fundamentals of Computer Science

In this interdisciplinary field of study, half of the classes taught me the fundamentals of computer science, and the other half communication science. I graduated within three years and was placed on the Dean's List twice. For my bachelor's thesis I carried out and reported an empirical quantitative study. The thesis was graded with an 1.0 (German grading system, equivalent to an A+) and was titled "The influence of user diversity on the awareness of filter bubbles regarding political opinion formation".

More information on this unique subject area can be found on the university's information page.

August 2009 - June 2015

Charlemagne College in Landgraaf

Bilingual University Preparatory Education (NL: tweetalig gymnasium)

After six years I graduated cum laude and was equipped with the necessary skills to attend a university, as well as a good mastery of the English language. In the Netherlands, bilingual secondary schools are regulated by Nuffic, a non-profit organisation that ensures the quality of the lessons and teachers. For me, following bilingual education meant that during the first half of secondary school, all classes, except for the languages, were taught in English. During the second half, lessons were taught in Dutch again, but I still had more hours of English a week, as well as additional projects and exams. Because of these, I was rewarded with several certificates: i.a., M15 English A Lang and Lit by the International Baccalaureate and English Language Advanced Subsidiary and Global Perspectives Advanced Subsidiary by Cambridge International Examinations.
